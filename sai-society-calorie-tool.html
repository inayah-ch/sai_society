ii.

Deficit, Maintenance, Surplus

Three states your body can be in, depending on calories in versus calories out. Your goal decides which one you want.

Eating below maintenance

Deficit

You eat fewer calories than your body burns in a day, so it pulls the difference from stored energy — mostly fat, if protein and training stay solid.

Goal: Lose body fat
Eating at balance

Maintenance

You eat roughly what your body burns in a day. Weight stays steady, and your training drives the change instead of your fork.

Goal: Get stronger / improve health
Eating above maintenance

Surplus

You eat more calories than your body burns, giving it the extra raw material it needs to build new muscle tissue.

Goal: Build muscle

Today's Stations

Five stations, working all three glute muscles — a well-rounded lower body session, start to finish.

Why this order: stations are sequenced from heavy compound movements to single-leg and isolation work. This lets you lift the most weight with good technique first, then finish by targeting stability and the smaller glute muscles as fatigue increases.
01
Hip Thrust 3 Sets · 6–8 Reps
Drive through your heels and squeeze at the top — this is the main lift of the day.
Glute Max Hamstrings
02
RDL (Romanian Deadlift) 3 Sets · 6–8 Reps
Hinge at the hips, soft knees, weight close to your legs the whole way down.
Glute Max Hamstrings
03
Step-Ups 3 Sets · 6–8 Reps Each Leg
Drive up through the front foot, control the descent — don't push off the back leg.
Glute Max Glute Medius Quads Balance & Stability
04
Side-Lying Weighted Hip Abduction 3 Sets · 6–8 Reps Each Side
Lift from the side of your hip, not your lower back — keep the movement slow and controlled.
Glute Medius Glute Minimus Hip Stabilizers
05
Cardio
Your finisher — keep effort steady and use it to bring your heart rate up before you cool down.
Conditioning
Things To Remember
01

Perform this workout 1–2 times a week — your glutes need recovery between sessions to actually grow.

02

Aim to gradually increase weight over time. Small, steady increases beat jumping too fast.

03

Eat around 1g of protein per pound of bodyweight every day — it's what your muscles actually rebuild from.

04

Focus on progress, not perfection. Showing up consistently beats any single perfect session.
